Search the taxonomy of the SACC classification.

Try typing any portion of a word, e.g., "xantho". You can include spaces, hyphens or accented characters. Capitalization is irrelevant.

When you click on a species in the results or taxonomy panel, the countries in which it occurs are highlighted on the continental map and in the taxonomic tree.

If nothing appears on the South America map, the species may occur in one of the islands covered by the SACC classification but not shown here, such as Bonaire or Aruba.

"extinct" refers to within the selected country only (or if searching in all of South America - extinct in at least one country), the species may occur in another country. "incertae [sedis]" refers to two "families" of uncertain classification.
